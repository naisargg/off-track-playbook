4 · The three forces behind the logos

Force 01 · GeographyHome markets still sign the checks

Ferrari's post-Santander bank is Italian (UniCredit); Mercedes runs a German stack (SAP, Adidas); Williams pairs British heritage brands like Gulf with its history; Haas and Cadillac anchor to American money — MoneyGram then Toyota's US racing arm, Tommy Hilfiger and GM. Atlassian buying Williams is the exception that proves it: software has no home race, so it buys heritage instead.

Force 02 · State & energy moneySoft power wears a livery

Petronas (Malaysia) and Aramco (Saudi Arabia) are state-linked energy giants whose deals are as much national positioning as marketing — Aramco's reportedly even carries a ~10% equity option[1]. Contrast Shell at Ferrari: seven decades of genuinely technical partnership, the oldest commercial relationship in the sport.

Force 03 · People & ownershipSponsors follow humans

The cleanest proof: Santander left Ferrari and followed Carlos Sainz to Williams in 2025[3] [4]. INEOS went from Mercedes sponsor to one-third owner. Red Bull and Haas Automation are the owners' own brands. Aston Martin exists in its current form because Lawrence Stroll built a team around his son and his brand portfolio.
