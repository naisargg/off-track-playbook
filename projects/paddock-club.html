Paddock Club Exclusivity Redesign

The Paddock Club is a high-margin B2B sales floor, not a catering operation — and its most common complaint from paying sponsors is exclusivity dilution at marquee races. This study answers the complaint with a product: a guest-stratification framework plus a B2B matchmaking app concept for race weekends.

1 · The problem, stated as a product spec

Hospitality is ~10% of F1 revenue and a core sponsorship deliverable — yet the people paying say marquee races traded the B2B room for reach. The fix isn't fewer guests; it's stratification with teeth: reach gets content moments, never the sales floor.

2 · The access matrix

TierWhoRightsCapacity rule
T1 · BoardroomTitle & principal partners' C-suiteTeam Principal one-on-one, pit-wall visit in practice, team-radio device, private dining~20 per race, never pooled
T2 · Partner executivePartner leadership & key accountsIn-session garage tour, engineer briefing, matchmaking floor access~80 per race
T3 · Commercial guestPartners' clients & prospectsPre-session paddock walk, hosted lounge, invited intros~150 per race
T4 · Reach guestMedia & creatorsDefined content windows, escorted routes — no B2B floor access≤10% of capacity; stricter at marquee races

The one rule that changes the room: reach and access never mix. Creators get better content moments than they get today (escorted garage windows are more filmable than a crowded lounge) — and the sales floor gets its scarcity back, which is the entire product.
