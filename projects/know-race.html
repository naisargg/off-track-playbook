The model is scarcity: shipments grew just 0.7% while revenue grew 11.8% — price, mix and personalization did the work. Roughly a fifth of car revenue is personalization. That is why EBITDA runs at 38% where volume carmakers run near 10%.

5 · The interview drill

Five questions that separate candidates from fans — answers folded for recall practice:

What does Ferrari actually earn, and on what?
FY2024: €6,677M revenue (+11.8%), €2,555M EBITDA at 38.3%, €1,526M net profit, €1,027M industrial free cash flow — on just 13,752 cars. Revenue is ~86% cars & spares; racing/sponsorship/brand is ~9%.
Why are the margins so high for a carmaker?
Because it isn't priced like a carmaker: supply is capped below demand, personalization adds ~a fifth of car revenue at rich margins, and the racing brand sustains pricing power. Shipments +0.7%, revenue +11.8% — that gap is the whole thesis.
How does the Scuderia make money?
Sponsorship (HP title at ~$100M/yr est., Shell, UniCredit, Puma, IBM), the team's share of F1's commercial revenues by championship position, and brand/licensing income. The segment's deeper job is protecting the price of every road car.
Where is Ferrari on electrification?
Hybrids are already ~half of shipments; the e-building (June 2024) builds ICE, hybrid and EV on one flexible line; the first EV's technology was shown at the October 2025 Capital Markets Day with launch staged into 2026; group carbon-neutrality is pledged for 2030.
Why do sponsors pay Ferrari nine figures?
The most-watched team in the most-watched motorsport, a luxury halo no other property offers, and enterprise-integration clauses — naming, co-engineering, B2B access. What gets activated versus wasted is exactly what Study 01 audits.
