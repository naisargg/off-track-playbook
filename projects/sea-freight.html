Sea-Freight Transition Simulator

F1 has committed to moving 50%+ of its broadcast-related freight off air transport by 2030, and logistics emissions are already down 29% versus the 2018 baseline. This simulator makes the remaining trade-offs tangible: an interactive map of the 2026 calendar where each flyaway race can be toggled between air, sea, and regional-hub strategies — with estimated CO₂, cost, and lead-time impacts.

What it will show

  • The actual 2026 race sequence, including the calendar rationalization moves (Canada before Monaco, Asia-Pacific clustering).
  • Per-leg modal choices and their consequences, using published freight-emissions conversion factors.
  • Why the modal shift is an operations problem, not just a sustainability pledge: duplicated equipment pools, months-ahead lead times, zero tolerance for missing a race.
Method: real freight costs are confidential — the simulator uses clearly-labeled estimates with a documented methodology. Sources: F1 published sustainability reports, public freight-emissions conversion factors, the 2026 calendar.
