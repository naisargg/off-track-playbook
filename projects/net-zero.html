Net Zero Progress Tracker

From a 2018 baseline of 228,793 tCO₂e, F1 has published a ~35% reduction — while growing from 21 to 24 races and from 4M to 6.5M attendees. This tracker turns the published numbers into a visual scorecard by category (logistics, travel, events, facilities), projected against the Net Zero 2030 target, with a scenario slider for the remaining gap.

What it will show

  • Category-level progress: facilities −64%, logistics −29%, all-European-GP alternative energy in 2025.
  • The growth-versus-reduction tension: cuts must outpace calendar and attendance expansion.
  • Where credibility risk lives: logistics is still 40–50% of the footprint, business travel another 25–30%.
Team framing: ESG strategy sits largely at FOM level — this study frames the team-side levers (factory emissions, travel, European truck legs) that a team like Ferrari or Audi/Sauber directly controls.
