2 · The four-quarter calendar

Melbourne → Bahrain — the debut window

The only season where "new" is the story: spend it

• Identity launch and livery reveal as a Revolut in-app event before Melbourne — measure app opens, not impressions.
• Limited debut card design; issuance is the first hard KPI of the deal.
• "Building a works team" content series from Hinwil and Neuburg — the one narrative no rival can copy.
• Measurement baseline wired: media-value tracking, per-asset attribution tags, the rights checklist at 0%.

Miami → European season — reach into the growth market

US races + home continent

• Miami consumer activation aimed at the 18–24, majority-female new-fan cohort — Revolut's exact acquisition target.
• German home-market push around the European leg; Neuburg engineering content for credibility.
• First B2B hospitality window at Monaco and Silverstone using the tiered access model.
• Quarter-end: first activation-completion reading against the rights inventory.

Summer → Singapore/Austin — convert attention to accounts

Reach is rented; accounts are owned

• Race-weekend cashback and FX-fee events in every flyaway market — sponsorship as product distribution.
• Per-market card drops for Singapore and Austin; geo-cohort signup attribution per race.
• Slow-scoreboard insurance: content keyed to the build story and 2027 pipeline, never to points scored.

Vegas → Abu Dhabi — the proof quarter

End the season with a defensible number

• Vegas flagship consumer moment; Abu Dhabi B2B closing program.
• Year-one QBR built on the four layers: exposure, attribution, completion rate, business impact.
• Target: ≥90% of contracted right categories activated with a public trace — beating the 83% the grid's second-biggest deal shows after two seasons (Study 01).
• Renewal-ready narrative drafted before anyone asks for it.

3 · Measurement — wired in before race one

LayerAudi-specific metricWhy it defends the fee
1 · ExposureMedia value per race, benchmarked against the ~$75M/yr feeThe floor every board understands
2 · AttributionPer-asset value: livery, drivers, in-app moments, card dropsShows which rights earn and which idle
3 · ExecutionActivation completion rate vs. the rights inventory aboveThe discipline metric — Study 01's core instrument
4 · Business impactApp signups and card issuance by race-market cohort; B2B pipeline from hospitalityRevolut can measure what most sponsors can't — the product is an app

The structural advantage: a fintech title sponsor is the rare case where layer 4 is directly measurable. Most teams sell reach; this partnership can report accounts opened per grand prix.
