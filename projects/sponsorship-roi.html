Flagship

Sponsorship Activation Audit & ROI Dashboard
Ferrari × HP

In April 2024 HP became Scuderia Ferrari's first title sponsor since the tobacco era, in a multi-year deal industry sources value at roughly $100M per year — the second-largest on the grid. Two seasons in: what has HP actually activated, what is still sitting in the contract, and how should a deal this size be measured?

3 · The gaps the audit found

Three hypotheses from the public record — the interesting part of any audit is what isn't there:

GapEvidenceWhy it matters
B2B channel activationNo public trace of Ferrari assets at HP Amplify — HP's flagship partner conference (2,200+ attendees) — in 2025 or 2026Classic "signed but never executed" clause; B2B access is what justifies nine-figure pricing to a board
Proof-of-value contentIBM — a smaller Ferrari partner — publishes a full case study on its Ferrari fan-app work; HP's equivalent enterprise storytelling is thinThe title sponsor is being out-published on ROI narrative by a partner paying a fraction of the fee
Measurement narrativePublic value evidence is still media-value counting (e.g. one Hamilton post ≈ $62,100 to HP, per Relo Metrics)Logo impressions can't defend a ~$100M line item — the measurement gap this project exists to close

4 · Proposed measurement framework

Four layers, in rising order of board-room defensibility:

LayerMetricData that exists today
1. ExposureMedia value (broadcast + social logo detection)Relo Metrics-style tracking; H1-2025 grid-wide sponsor media value was $665M
2. AttributionSocial attribution per asset (driver posts, special liveries, esports)Per-post value figures already exist publicly for Ferrari content
3. ExecutionActivation completion rate — % of contracted rights actually deployed each seasonThis audit's inventory is the numerator; the contract is the denominator
4. Business impactB2B pipeline proxy (partner-event meetings, co-sell motions, Ferrari-badged product revenue)HP.com storefronts and the 2026 co-engineered PCs make this measurable
